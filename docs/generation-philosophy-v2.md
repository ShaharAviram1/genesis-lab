## 1. Design Direction Pivot

The previous generation structure drifted toward a biology simulation wrapped in chemistry clothing. Gen 3 was prebiotic chemistry. Gen 4 was life systems. Gen 5 was civilization management. The reactor was the input mechanism for content that lived elsewhere — in metabolic cycles, protein folding, DNA, Bronze Age progression.

**That direction is abandoned.**

Genesis Lab is a reactor-centric synthesis game. The reactor is not a means to an end. It is the end. Every generation must make the reactor feel more powerful, more impossible, more mythic. Players are not building a civilization or simulating biology. They are operating a machine that gradually becomes something that should not exist.

The new progression arc is:

```
matter  →  chemistry  →  advanced materials  →  extreme states  →  cosmic alchemy  →  reality manipulation
```

This is not a simulation of history. It is an escalation of reactor power.

---

## 2. What Genesis Lab Is Not

These are design constraints, not preferences. Content that falls into these categories should be cut or reframed.

**Not a biology sandbox.**  
No amino acid chains. No nucleobases. No DNA/RNA synthesis. No ATP metabolism. No cell membranes. No proteins as a primary progression arc. Biology may appear as flavor in lore text, but never as a gameplay system. The reactor does not simulate cells.

**Not a civilization simulator.**  
No Bronze Age narrative. No gunpowder for historical reasons. No saltpeter. No cement as a "construction material." No pharmaceutical development arc. No progression framed as human eras. The reactor does not simulate human history.

**Not a planet simulator.**  
No geology-first framing. The reactor is not building a planet. Mineral compounds (SiO₂, CaCO₃, etc.) are valuable because they feed synthesis chains, not because they model geological processes.

**Not a chemistry encyclopedia.**  
No exhaustive periodic table coverage. No completionism across all organic compound families. Every substance must have a gameplay identity, a fantasy identity, and a clear progression purpose. A substance that exists only to fill a chemical category is wrong.

This means actively collapsing invisible intermediates. If three substances in a row each have a single use and no emotional weight, they should be one substance. If an intermediate compound exists only to be stepped over on the way to the next compound — no player moment attached, no identity, no shard value, no discovery — it should be eliminated or absorbed into the adjacent reaction. Realism is inspiration. Gameplay density is the priority.

**Not a factory map game.**  
No logistics. No pipeline maps. No colony administration. The reactor is a single machine. The player's relationship is with that machine, not with a network of machines.

**Not a wizard simulator.**  
The mythic substances of Gen 6 are impossible. They must still feel synthesized, not conjured. Scientific adjacency is not optional for Gen 6 content — it is the test that separates legitimate cosmic alchemy from fantasy nonsense. An exotic substance with no connection to real physics is lore filler. An exotic substance whose name comes from alchemy but whose synthesis chain runs through particle physics and quantum mechanics is Genesis Lab. The reactor must always feel like an impossible machine. Never like a wizard artifact.

## 3. The New Progression Arc

```
Gen 1 — Fundamental Matter       The reactor wakes. Energy becomes matter.
Gen 2 — Industrial Synthesis     The reactor burns. Matter becomes compounds.
Gen 3 — Advanced Materials       The reactor creates. Compounds become designer matter.
Gen 4 — Extreme States           The reactor reaches. The rules begin to bend.
Gen 5 — Cosmic Alchemy           The reactor transcends. The impossible becomes synthesis.
Gen 6 — Reality Manipulation     The reactor becomes. Myth is made real.
```

At Gen 1, the reactor is a scientific instrument. At Gen 6, it is something that should not exist. The transition between these states is the game.

## 4. Generation Philosophy — Revised

---

### Gen 1 — Fundamental Matter

**Theme:** The first synthesis. Energy becomes matter. Matter becomes molecules.

**Gameplay Fantasy:** You are learning to speak the language of the universe. The reactor is precise, controlled, and young. Every discovery — even water — feels like an act of creation.

**Reactor Feel:** Scientific. Clean. The glow is pale blue. Reactions are crisp. The core hums steadily. This is a machine built by physics.

**Substance Direction:**  
Light elements created from energy. Simple inorganic compounds. First redox demonstrations. The substance pool is small, clean, and foundational. Nothing here requires more than 2 reactants. Everything here will be used again in a later generation.

**Substance Examples:**
- Elements: Hydrogen, Helium, Carbon, Nitrogen, Oxygen, Sodium, Chlorine, Iron, Sulfur, Argon
- Compounds: Water, Carbon dioxide, Carbon monoxide, Ammonia, Methane, Hydrogen chloride, Sodium chloride, Iron oxide, Hydrogen sulfide, Nitric oxide

**Milestone Substances:**
- *Sodium chloride* — first Tier advance; the first salt, the first discovery that earns a reaction panel entry
- *Ammonia* — the nitrogen gateway; everything in every later generation that uses nitrogen chains through here
- *Iron oxide* — first redox; teaches the core mechanism used in Gen 3 steel chain

**What Is Different from v1:**  
No change to Gen 1 substance list. The changes are in what Gen 1 leads to. Hydrogen sulfide no longer leads to amino acids. Ammonia no longer leads to nucleobases. These elements are re-routed to advanced materials and industrial synthesis chains. The dependency graph of Gen 1 substances is preserved — their downstream purpose changes.

### Gen 2 — Industrial Synthesis

**Theme:** Mastery of the reactor as forge. Pressure, acid, reduction.

**Gameplay Fantasy:** You are an industrial alchemist. The reactor is running hot. You are producing compounds that required centuries of human chemistry to understand, and you are doing it in a single machine. The scale is expanding. The reactions are more demanding. The inventory is getting complex.

**Reactor Feel:** Hotter. More intense. The core glows amber-orange at high activity. Industrial — but still grounded in recognizable science. This is a machine at high temperature.

**Substance Direction:**  
The important metals and metalloids unlock (Si, Ca, Al, Cu, Sn, etc.). Strong acids and bases are the first milestone reactions — sulfuric acid, nitric acid, sodium hydroxide. Simple organics begin (formaldehyde, acetic acid, methanol) as feedstock for Gen 3, not as biological precursors. The first alloy (bronze) demonstrates the new `alloy` reaction type. Glass and cement demonstrate multi-reactant synthesis. The framing is industrial mastery, not planetary geology.

**Substance Examples:**
- New elements: Silicon, Phosphorus, Calcium, Aluminum, Copper, Tin, Zinc, Gold, Lithium, Nickel, Fluorine
- Industrial compounds: Sulfuric acid, Nitric acid, Phosphoric acid, Sodium hydroxide, Calcium oxide, Silicon dioxide, Sodium carbonate
- Simple organics: Formaldehyde, Acetic acid, Methanol, Urea
- Alloys: Bronze

**Milestone Substances:**
- *Sulfuric acid* — the "king of chemicals." H₂SO₄ is the first industrial-scale milestone. Its chain (S → SO₂ → SO₃ → H₂SO₄) teaches multi-step synthesis. High shard value. Tier gate.
- *Bronze* — first alloy type. The synthesis reaction type changes here. Visually distinct as an alloy.

**What Is Cut from v1:**  
`potassium_nitrate` / gunpowder removed — civilization framing.  
`calcium_phosphate` removed — existed only to feed bone matrix (Gen 4 biology, now removed).  
`hydrogen_cyanide` removed — existed only to feed amino acid chains (now removed).  
`calcium_carbonate` kept but reframed as cement feedstock, not geology simulation.  
`sodium_carbonate` kept but Solvay Process framing is reactor synthesis, not industrial history.

### Gen 3 — Advanced Materials

**Theme:** The reactor as precision forge. Creating matter that nature cannot.

**Gameplay Fantasy:** You are a synthesist building designer matter. Graphene. Steel. Carbon fiber. High-purity silicon. These are not compounds that exist in river sediment — they are substances that require the reactor's exact conditions to produce. The player's first encounter with something that feels engineered, not discovered.

**Reactor Feel:** Pulsing. Deliberate. Each reaction feels intentional. The core shifts to cooler blue-white during carbon synthesis. The rings move with more precision. The motes are denser, more purposeful. This reactor is creating things.

**Substance Direction:**  
The steel chain is here (simplified to 3 steps: iron oxide → iron + coke → steel). Graphene and carbon fiber anchor the advanced carbon family. The semiconductor chain begins (SiO₂ → pure silicon → doped silicon). Synthetic polymers are included but compressed — polyethylene and one high-performance polymer, not an encyclopedic set. New elements unlock (Titanium, Chromium, Tungsten, Boron). The framing is precision synthesis, not civilization-era progression.

**Substance Examples:**
- New elements: Titanium, Chromium, Tungsten, Boron
- Metals and alloys: Steel, Stainless steel, Titanium alloy, Tungsten carbide, Osmium-iridium alloy
- Advanced carbons: Graphene, Fullerene (C₆₀), Carbon nanotube, Carbon fiber
- Polymers: Ethylene, Polyethylene, High-performance polymer
- Semiconductor chain: Pure silicon, Doped silicon (n-type and p-type)
- Precision glass: Borosilicate glass, Fused quartz

**Milestone Substances:**
- *Steel* — the industrial milestone of Gen 3. Requires two earlier intermediates. High shard value. The moment the player produces steel, the reactor feels different.
- *Graphene* — single-atom carbon sheet. Should feel impossible to make. Very high energy cost. Gate to Gen 4 and Gen 5 chains. The "wonder material" milestone.
- *Doped silicon* — the semiconductor milestone. Converges silicon, boron, and phosphorus chains. Opens the battery and electronics system.

**What Replaces Old Gen 3 (Prebiotic Chemistry):**  
The entire prebiotic biology arc is removed: glycine, alanine, cysteine, serine, aspartic acid, glutamic acid, lysine, adenine, cytosine, guanine, uracil, ribose, deoxyribose, pyruvate, citric acid — all removed from the content universe.

The organic compounds that survive from old Gen 2/3 (formaldehyde, acetic acid, urea) survive only because they have non-biological uses in the advanced materials chain: urea for carbon-nitrogen composite synthesis, acetic acid as an industrial solvent/reactant, formaldehyde for polymer feedstock.

Phosphorus survives in Gen 2 as a semiconductor dopant (phosphoric acid → doped silicon), not as a biological phosphate backbone.

### Gen 4 — Extreme States

**Theme:** The reactor reaches beyond normal conditions. Physics bends.

**Gameplay Fantasy:** The reactor is achieving states that do not occur naturally on Earth's surface. Plasma. Metallic hydrogen. Superconductors. Dense phase carbon synthesized, not geological. The player is operating a machine that is approaching the edge of what matter can be. The rules still apply — barely.

**Reactor Feel:** Alive. Intense. The core begins cycling through colors that feel almost wrong — deep violet, electromagnetic blue, brief white-hot pulses. The ring rotation changes quality. Motes move in patterns that feel non-classical. Something is changing in the reactor. The player can feel it.

**Substance Direction:**  
Plasma states are the opening of Gen 4. The reactor can now sustain plasma — hydrogen plasma first, then multi-element reactive plasma. This is a new physical category. Superconductors emerge from the silicon and metal chains — ceramic superconductor materials require aluminum oxide and yttrium (a new element). Metallic hydrogen requires extreme pressure conditions — first use of the `conditions` field as a real gameplay gate. Nuclear chemistry begins here with uranium unlocking.

**Substance Examples:**
- Plasma states: Hydrogen plasma, Reactive plasma core (multi-element)
- Dense forms: Diamond (synthetic), Metallic hydrogen
- Superconductors: YBCO ceramic superconductor, Niobium-titanium alloy
- Exotic alloys: Osmium-iridium composite, Rhenium superalloy, Inconel variant
- Nuclear: Uranium, Uranium oxide, Nuclear fuel pellet, Lithium deuteride (fusion fuel precursor)

**Milestone Substances:**
- *Reactive plasma core* — the first plasma-state substance produced in the reactor. The reactor animation changes visibly when a plasma core is created. This is the moment the reactor starts feeling alive. Gen 4 tier gate.
- *YBCO superconductor* — a convergence of four prior chains (aluminum oxide, yttrium, copper, barium). Demonstrates the "T-junction" synthesis design philosophy.
- *Nuclear fuel pellet* — the energy gateway to Gen 5. Required as an energy input for fusion reactions.

**What Replaces Old Gen 4 (Life Systems):**  
The entire life systems arc is removed: ATP, ADP, AMP, NAD⁺, acetyl-CoA, phospholipid, cell membrane, DNA strand, RNA strand, collagen, hemoglobin, chlorophyll, starch, cellulose, natural rubber, ethanol (as fermentation product), lactic acid — all removed.

Ethanol survives only as a synthetic feedstock (methanol catalytic chain → ethanol → ethylene → polyethylene). Its fermentation origin is removed. The biological framing is replaced with synthetic industrial framing.

The automation concept that was tied to enzymes and biological catalysts is reframed: automation in the new system is unlocked by the production of the first *plasma* state substance — the reactor maintaining plasma is the thematic equivalent. This is conceptually stronger: it's reactor-automation, not biological-automation.

### Gen 5 — Cosmic Alchemy

**Theme:** The reactor crosses into particle physics. The impossible becomes synthesis. The reactor is already terrifying before myth arrives.

**Gameplay Fantasy:** You are operating something that should not exist. Antimatter. Strange matter. Quantum foam condensate. The substances you are creating appear in nature only in neutron star collisions and the first microseconds after the Big Bang. The reactor is producing them. Methodically. In your inventory. The science is real. The context is impossible.

**Reactor Feel:** Otherworldly. The 3D scene begins doing things that feel non-physical — colors shift through spectra that don't exist in normal chemistry, the core pulses with a heartbeat that feels cosmological. The reactor is barely a machine. It is a contained cosmic event.

**Substance Direction:**  
Nuclear isotopes unlock: deuterium, tritium, helium-3. Fusion reactions become possible, producing fusion plasma with enormous positive energyChange — the reactor's energy mechanics change meaningfully here. The antimatter chain begins: nuclear fuel pellet enables pair production, yielding positrons; fusion plasma enables antiproton synthesis; combining these produces antihydrogen, the Gen 5 antimatter milestone. Alongside antimatter, two parallel chains define Gen 5's emotional shape: the stellar degenerate matter chain, which culminates in the Stellar Core Fragment milestone; and the extreme gravity chain, which culminates in the Event Horizon Condensate. These two substances are Gen 5's iconic anchors — the moments players remember before the game crosses into Gen 6.

**Substance Examples:**
- Nuclear isotopes: Deuterium, Tritium, Helium-3
- Fusion products: Fusion plasma, Helium-3 (byproduct)
- Antimatter chain: Positron, Antiproton, Antihydrogen
- Stellar degenerate matter: Neutron star fragment, Stellar Core Fragment
- Extreme gravity states: Quantum collapse precursor, Event Horizon Condensate
- Exotic matter: Strange quark condensate, Dark matter proxy, Quantum foam lattice

**Milestone Substances:**
- *Fusion plasma* — the Gen 5 energy breakthrough. Enormous positive energyChange. The reactor produces stars-in-miniature. This changes how the economy works.
- *Antihydrogen* — the antimatter culmination. A complete antimatter atom produced in the reactor. Requires three prior chain steps. Matter-antimatter annihilation produces the highest energyChange event in the game.
- *Stellar Core Fragment* — **Gen 5's first iconic anchor.** A stabilized pellet of neutron-degenerate matter synthesized by the reactor under conditions that replicate the interior of a dying star. Neutron degeneracy pressure is real physics — this extends it to a reactor context. The player is holding, in their inventory, something that exists inside neutron stars. It is not mythic. It is scientific. But it feels like nothing the player has made before. Very high shard value. High energyCost. Low yield. Its discovery should be a moment the player pauses.
- *Event Horizon Condensate* — **Gen 5's second iconic anchor.** A stable condensate of matter that has passed through extreme spacetime curvature — gravitationally collapsed matter held in suspension by the reactor's containment field. This sits at the edge of black hole physics without crossing into myth. The reactor is simulating conditions at the event horizon. The substance that results should not exist in the lab. It does. Gen 5 final tier gate. Requires Stellar Core Fragment as a reactant. The visual when this is synthesized should be unforgettable — and should feel like a natural escalation from everything the player has done, not like a genre switch.

**The Continuity Principle — Gen 5 to Gen 6:**  
Gen 6 must feel like the inevitable next step from Gen 5, not a genre change. The player who has synthesized an Event Horizon Condensate has already crossed a conceptual threshold — they have accepted that the reactor operates outside normal physics. Gen 6 takes that acceptance and names it. The Philosopher's Stone is not more fantastical than the Event Horizon Condensate. It is just older, and its name comes from human longing rather than human physics. The player should experience Gen 6 as: "Of course. Of course this is what comes next." If the transition from Gen 5 to Gen 6 feels like a surprise, the Gen 5 content has not done its job.

### Gen 6 — Reality Manipulation

**Theme:** The reactor creates substances that should not exist. Myth is made real.

**Gameplay Fantasy:** The reactor is no longer a machine. It is a legend given physical form. Every synthesis feels miraculous. The substances have names from alchemy and metaphysics — not because the game is fantasy, but because these substances are so far beyond normal matter that only mythic language can describe them. The player has earned the right to make the impossible.

**Reactor Feel:** Transcendent. The 3D scene looks fundamentally different from Gen 1 — the space around the reactor appears distorted, the core glows with colors that feel like they are from outside the visible spectrum, the rings move as if they are orbiting something larger than themselves. Each reaction event in Gen 6 produces an animation that resembles a cosmic event more than a chemistry experiment. Players who have seen the Gen 1 water discovery and now see a Philosopher's Stone synthesis will understand how far they have come.

**Substance Direction:**  
Gen 6 has a maximum of 8–10 substances. Every one of them is a prestige artifact. None can be mass-produced. Every synthesis requires multiple Gen 5 chain convergences. All Gen 6 reactions are hidden — no discovery hints of the "you're missing a substance" variety. The hint system at Gen 6 is evocative and cryptic, not prescriptive. The player must experiment with the impossible.

**The Naming Philosophy:**  
Gen 6 substance names are drawn from real alchemy, classical physics, and metaphysics. They are not invented fantasy words. They resonate immediately because they carry centuries of human longing attached to them. Players do not need to be told what the Philosopher's Stone is. They already feel it.

Each substance has a scientific adjacency — a real concept it extends past the edge of:

| Substance | Scientific Adjacency | Extension Into Myth |
|---|---|---|
| Prima Materia | Quantum vacuum, zero-point field | The substrate from which all matter emerges — "first matter" |
| Philosopher's Stone | Catalytic transmutation, autocatalysis | A substance that transforms other substances; changes the reaction palette |
| Aether | Higgs field, dark energy, electromagnetic medium | A medium that propagates things which should have no medium |
| Neutron Glass | Neutron star crust crystallography | A crystalline form of neutron matter held stable outside its origin |
| Void Crystal | Casimir effect, vacuum energy | A crystallized unit of vacuum energy — matter made from nothing |
| False Vacuum Seed | Quantum phase transition, vacuum metastability | A seed that could, theoretically, rewrite the laws of the local universe |
| Chrono Dust | Time dilation, exotic particle decay rates | Matter whose internal clock runs at a different rate than normal spacetime |
| Dark Matter Crystal | Dark matter particle physics | Crystallized dark matter — the substance that makes up most of the universe, now in inventory |

**Milestone Substances:**
- *Prima Materia* — the first Gen 6 substance. The "first matter." Requires multiple Gen 5 convergences. Its synthesis is the moment the reactor crosses from cosmic alchemy into reality manipulation. It acts as the base input for most other Gen 6 reactions.
- *Philosopher's Stone* — the Gen 6 apex catalyst. It does not just have high shard value — it changes the reactor. Producing the Philosopher's Stone unlocks a new reaction type: `transmutation_mythic`, which allows certain Gen 1–3 substances to be transmuted directly into higher-tier outputs. This is the one Gen 6 substance that has an active mechanical effect beyond shards.
- *Dark Matter Crystal* — the final prestige substance. The last entry in the content universe. Its shard value is designed to be worth more than a complete Gen 1–4 run. Producing it should feel like an achievement the player wants to tell someone about.

**Exclusions:**  
Gen 6 must not become a random list of exotic-sounding words. Every substance must have: a mythic name with historical resonance, a scientific adjacency that makes it feel reactor-created, and a clear chain dependency from Gen 5 substances. If a proposed Gen 6 substance fails any of these three tests, it does not belong.

## 5. The Reality Gradient

The reality gradient is the tonal arc that runs beneath the entire game. It is the master tonal guide for all systems — visuals, sound, hint language, reaction behavior, and UI. As the player advances, the world of Genesis Lab gradually becomes less stable, less scientific, and more mythic. This transition must be continuous — never a sudden jump, never a genre change.

### Core Gradient Table

| Gen | The Reactor Is | The Rules | Discovery Feels Like | Visuals | Discovery Language |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | A scientific instrument | Chemistry textbook applies | An experiment succeeding | Clean pale blue glow; crisp particle behavior; colors match real chemistry | "The reaction formed successfully." |
| 2 | An industrial forge | Engineering applies | Mastery over a process | Amber-orange heat glow; denser motes; more energetic ring motion | "A new industrial compound crystallized." |
| 3 | A precision synthesizer | Materials science applies | Building something nature cannot | Blue-white precision light; rings move with geometric exactness; motes orbit tightly | "The reactor has produced designer matter." |
| 4 | An extreme state chamber | Physics applies, barely | Achieving something that requires stars | Colors shift into impossible frequencies; electromagnetic blue-violet pulses; space around core begins to distort slightly | "Conditions exceeded. An extreme state stabilized." |
| 5 | A cosmic event, barely contained | Particle physics applies, at the extreme edge | Producing something that exists only in theory | Core glows with colors outside normal spectra; rings leave light trails; motes behave non-classically | "The reactor has crossed a known threshold." |
| 6 | A reality engine | The rules do not apply | A miracle | Scene appears fundamentally distorted; core light feels volumetric and wrong; space around reactor warps visibly | "Something ancient has formed." |

### System-by-System Gradient

**Visuals — core and scene:**  
Gen 1 scene colors match real-world chemistry. Iron oxide is red. Water is near-colorless. By Gen 3, synthesis produces light that feels engineered — crystalline, precise. By Gen 4, the core begins emitting colors that do not correspond to any atomic emission spectrum. By Gen 5, the 3D space around the reactor begins to feel gravitationally distorted. Gen 6 scenes should look like nothing else in the game — not because the assets are different, but because the color, intensity, and behavior of every visual element has drifted from normal physics.

**Sound:**  
Gen 1 sounds are mechanical and crisp — the reactor hums, reactions click and resolve cleanly. By Gen 2, the reactor carries a deeper industrial resonance. Gen 3 adds precision tones — high-frequency harmonics on synthesis reactions. Gen 4 plasma reactions should sound like something powering up that shouldn't be powering up. Gen 5 fusion events should feel like a contained detonation. Gen 6 reactions are near-silent — an absence of sound that feels worse than noise, followed by something that doesn't fit any category of sound the player has heard before.

**Reaction behavior:**  
Gen 1–2: reactions complete cleanly and quickly. Success is clear. Failure is clear. Gen 3: some reactions have longer buildup phases — the reactor has to reach conditions before synthesis begins. Gen 4: `conditions` field becomes active gameplay; some reactions require other substances to be in specific states first. Gen 5: reaction timing becomes longer and less predictable — the physics are at the edge. Gen 6: reactions have irregular timing, high failure rates, and evocative rather than descriptive fail messages. The behavior of the synthesis itself communicates that the reactor is working outside its designed envelope.

**Discovery language — hint system:**  
The hint system's voice shifts with the gradient. Gen 1: "You may be missing an oxidizer." Gen 2: "This reaction requires more intense conditions." Gen 3: "The reactor lacks a precision input for this synthesis." Gen 4: "The conditions required exceed current plasma stability." Gen 5: "This substance exists only at the edge of the known." Gen 6: "The reactor senses an edge between states. Something very old wants to form." The player should be able to tell which generation they are in from the hint language alone.

**UI tone:**  
Gen 1 UI is clinical and informative — substance names are precise, reaction labels are direct. By Gen 3, substance names start carrying more weight — "Graphene" feels different from "Sodium chloride." By Gen 5, substance names like "Antihydrogen" and "Strange quark condensate" create a register shift in how the player reads the UI. Gen 6 substance names in the reaction panel should feel like they do not belong in a scientific interface — and that feeling is correct.

## 6. Economy Fields — Conceptual Distinction

**This section does not define the economy. It establishes how the economy's fields should be conceptually separated before balance work begins.**

The current schema has a single `baseEnergy` field that is used ambiguously — sometimes meaning the cost to create an atom, sometimes implying how energetically significant a substance is, sometimes loosely correlating with prestige importance. This conflation is a design liability. Before economy balance begins, the following three concepts should be treated as distinct:

**`atomCreationCost`**  
The energy consumed from the reactor to create one unit of this element via the Atom Panel. Only relevant for base elements. Higher for heavier, rarer elements. This is a gate and a resource drain — it governs how aggressively the player can source raw materials.

**`energyDensity`**  
The energy value a substance contributes to the reactor when held or processed — its role in the energy economy during synthesis. This governs whether holding a substance in inventory contributes to reactor capacity, and how much. This is separate from creation cost: a substance can be cheap to create but energy-rich (methane), or expensive to create but energy-neutral (gold).

**`shardValue`**  
The prestige value awarded per BigBang for having produced this substance. This is a statement about the substance's importance in the game's long-term arc. It should scale with the emotional and progression weight of the substance — not with its energyCost or atomCreationCost. A Gen 6 substance with no energyDensity and a low atomCreationCost may still carry an enormous shardValue because of its prestige position in the progression.

These three systems serve different gameplay purposes:
- `atomCreationCost` governs pacing and resource management
- `energyDensity` governs the reactor economy and synthesis throughput
- `shardValue` governs long-term prestige progression and player motivation

They should be designed independently. Conflating them produces substances that are expensive to make, poor energy sources, and poor shard generators simultaneously — which creates progression dead zones. The economy balance pass should treat each field as its own design axis.

## 7. Reactivity — Field Philosophy

The `reactivity` field on the Substance schema describes a substance's eagerness to react, its chemical volatility, and its instability under reactor conditions. It does **not** describe how many reactions happen to use it.

This distinction is critical. Conflating reactivity with recipe count creates a circular design: substances are flagged as reactive because they appear in many reactions, then designed into many reactions because they are flagged as reactive. This generates filler.

**Reactivity means:**
- How readily this substance transforms under reactor energy input
- How unstable its bonds are at reactor conditions
- How volatile or dangerous it is in contact with other substances
- How dramatically it behaves when conditions change

**Reactivity does not mean:**
- "This substance has 15 reaction recipes"
- "This substance is useful in many chains"
- "This substance is important to progression"

A substance can have extremely high reactivity and only two recipes (fluorine — it reacts with almost everything, but we only author the reactions that serve the game). A substance can have low reactivity and appear in five recipes (gold — it does not want to react, but it can be used in various precision processes under specific conditions).

**Design direction:** Set `reactivity` values from the substance's actual chemical character, then let the number of authored recipes follow from what gameplay requires. Never derive `reactivity` from recipe count. The hint system uses `reactivity` to calibrate experiment feedback — a high-reactivity substance will generate stronger hints about failed experiments because its nature wants to react. This only works if `reactivity` reflects genuine chemical volatility, not editorial prominence.

## 8. Mythic Substance Design Philosophy

These are the design rules for Gen 6 substances. They apply to no other generation.

**Rule 1: Named from history.**  
Every Gen 6 substance must have a name that existed before Genesis Lab. Philosopher's Stone, Prima Materia, Aether — these names carry weight because humans have dreamed about them for centuries. Made-up names (e.g., "Xenocryst," "Voidite") have none of this weight. Do not invent. Borrow.

**Rule 2: Scientifically adjacent.**  
Every Gen 6 substance must connect to a real scientific concept, even if it exceeds that concept by orders of magnitude. Void Crystal → Casimir effect (real). False Vacuum Seed → vacuum metastability (real). Chrono Dust → time dilation (real). This keeps them feeling reactor-created, not fantasy-invented. The chain from Gen 5 inputs to Gen 6 output must trace through physics — not through lore. The reactor synthesizes these substances. It does not summon them.

**Rule 3: Convergence required.**  
No Gen 6 substance can be synthesized from a straight-line chain. Every Gen 6 substance requires at least two Gen 5 chains to converge at a T-junction synthesis. The player must have invested in multiple paths — the mythic substance is the reward for breadth.

**Rule 4: Low yield, no mass production, no automation.**  
Every Gen 6 synthesis produces 1 unit. The player cannot stockpile. Gen 6 is not an idle resource tier. No mythic substance should ever become a passive generator output, feed a "+N/sec" economy loop, or be produced by automation. If the Philosopher's Stone becomes routine, it has failed. If the player is producing Dark Matter Crystals on a tick, the design has failed. Scarcity is not a balance parameter. It is the emotional foundation of the Gen 6 experience. The economy systems must be deliberately structured to prevent mythic substances from entering production loops — ever.

**Rule 5: Discovery only, evocative hints.**  
All Gen 6 reactions are `discoveredByDefault: false`. The hint system in Gen 6 delivers evocative suggestion, not chemistry clues. The player experiments with the impossible and arrives at the mythic through intuition and iteration, not systematic elimination.

**Rule 6: Shard value is exponential.**  
The shard economy of Gen 6 is nonlinear by design. A single Dark Matter Crystal is worth more shards than every Gen 1 substance combined. This is intentional — Gen 6 is the prestige endgame, and reaching it should transform the player's prestige trajectory permanently.

**Rule 7: The Philosopher's Stone is special.**  
Of all Gen 6 substances, the Philosopher's Stone alone has an active mechanical effect. It unlocks the `transmutation_mythic` reaction type, allowing certain substances from lower generations to be transformed directly into higher-tier outputs. This is not magic — it is the ultimate expression of the reactor's synthesis power, and it uses the same reaction system as everything else. It just does something no prior reaction type could do.

**Rule 8: Every synthesis should be memorable.**  
The moment a player produces a Gen 6 substance, the reactor should respond in a way that communicates the scale of what just happened. This is not just visual — it is pacing. Gen 6 reactions should have longer synthesis times, more dramatic animations, and a discovery moment that feels categorically different from Gen 1–5 discoveries. The player should not produce a Void Crystal and then immediately wonder what to do next. They should sit with it for a moment.

---

## 9. Big Bang as Mythic Escalation

In the previous design, each BigBang was a neutral prestige reset. In v2, each BigBang is a step in the reactor's transformation.

The BigBang sequence is not just an economy reset. It is the reactor consuming itself and being reborn with more power and less restraint. The reactor after BigBang 5 should feel nothing like the reactor at BigBang 0 — not just in substances available, but in how the scene looks and feels.

**Generation Unlock Gates:**

| BigBang | Unlocks | Narrative |
|---|---|---|
| Start | Gen 1 | The reactor initializes. |
| BigBang 1 | Gen 2 | The reactor reboots hotter. Industrial synthesis is now possible. |
| BigBang 2 | Gen 3 | The reactor burns with new intensity. Advanced materials unlock. |
| BigBang 3 | Gen 4 | Something changes in the reactor. It reaches extreme states. |
| BigBang 4 | Gen 5 | The reactor crosses a threshold. Cosmic alchemy begins. |
| BigBang 5+ | Gen 6 accessible | The reactor has become something else. Myth is reachable. |

**BigBang Escalation:**  
Each BigBang animation should be more dramatic than the last. BigBang 1 is a scientific collapse-and-reboot. BigBang 3 feels like something going wrong in a beautiful way. BigBang 5 should feel like a cosmic event. The same animation system serves all of them — but the reactor's accumulated power changes how it manifests.

**Shards as Narrative:**  
The shard economy maps onto the reality gradient. Gen 1 shards are the beginning of a run's momentum. Gen 6 shards are transformative — a single run reaching antihydrogen can fund multiple prestige upgrades. This creates a feedback loop where reaching Gen 5 accelerates future Gen 5 runs, which accelerates Gen 6 access.

**Multiple BigBangs Within Gen 6:**  
Gen 6 is designed to require multiple BigBang cycles to fully explore. Not because the player is grinding — but because Gen 6 discovery is intentionally difficult, and the player may need to BigBang back to Gen 5 to accumulate more specific inputs. This is intended. Gen 6 is not a single achievement. It is a territory the player explores over many cycles.

## 10. Reactor Evolution Philosophy

The reactor is not static. As the player advances through generations, the reactor changes — not just the substances it can produce, but its identity.

**Gen 1:** The reactor is a scientific instrument. The player is its operator.

**Gen 2:** The reactor is an industrial forge. The player is its engineer.

**Gen 3:** The reactor is a precision synthesizer. The player is its designer.

**Gen 4:** The reactor is an extreme state chamber. The player is its controller — barely.

**Gen 5:** The reactor is a cosmic event. The player and the reactor are collaborators in something dangerous.

**Gen 6:** The reactor is a mythic engine. The player has become an alchemist. The reactor has become a legend.

This evolution must be reflected in the 3D scene. The core, rings, and motes should feel meaningfully different between Gen 1 and Gen 6 — not in model assets necessarily, but in behavior, color, intensity, animation character. A Gen 6 reactor should look at a Gen 1 reactor the way a star looks at a candle.

**The reactor is the emotional center of the game — always.**  
Every major system in Genesis Lab must be evaluated against a single question: does this deepen the player's relationship with the reactor, or does it replace it? Automation systems, upgrade trees, reaction logs, prestige mechanics — each of these is justified only by how they change the way the player interacts with the reactor. If a future system occupies the player's attention for extended periods without the reactor being involved — if the player is spending time in menus, maps, or management interfaces that have nothing to do with the synthesis loop — that system likely does not belong. The reactor is not one feature among many. It is the game.

## 11. Design Principles for the New Universe

**The reactor is always the protagonist.** Every substance, reaction, and system exists to deepen the player's relationship with the reactor. If content pulls the player's attention away from the reactor for extended periods, it does not belong.

**Depth through chemistry, not breadth through encyclopedia.** Prefer 3 substances that each have 4 uses over 12 substances that each have 1 use. Interconnection is what makes chains feel alive.

**Collapse invisible intermediates.** The player's attention is a resource. If an intermediate substance has no gameplay identity — no emotional weight, no strategic decision, no memorable discovery — it should be eliminated or absorbed into the adjacent reaction. Three substances each serving one purpose is worse design than one substance serving three. The willingness to cut and merge is what separates a designed game from a chemistry database.

**Every substance has a fantasy identity.** Players remember substances by how they feel, not by their formula. "Iron oxide" is recognizable. "Reactive plasma core" is exciting. "Philosopher's Stone" is mythic. Every substance should have a name that communicates its fantasy role instantly. If a substance has no fantasy identity, it has no place in the content universe.

**Reactivity is volatility, not recipe count.** Set `reactivity` values from the substance's genuine chemical character — its instability, eagerness to transform, and reactor-condition behavior. Then let the number of authored reactions follow from gameplay requirements. Never derive a reactivity value from how many recipes a substance appears in. A substance can be maximally reactive and appear in two reactions. A substance can appear in six reactions and be nearly inert.

**Chemistry authenticity is inspiration, not obligation.** The game draws on real chemistry for substance identity, reaction plausibility, and tonal grounding. It is not bound to reproduce real chemistry accurately. When real chemistry would produce invisible intermediates, encyclopedic compound families, or substances with no emotional weight, the game is allowed — and expected — to simplify, merge, or invent within its design principles.

**The escalation must be felt.** The shift from Gen 1 to Gen 6 must be felt by the player through every system — animations, colors, reaction types, discovery hints, shard values, and the 3D scene. If a player who has reached Gen 6 still feels like they are doing the same thing they did in Gen 1, the escalation has failed.

**Scarcity creates prestige.** Gen 6 substances must be rare by design. If they can be mass-produced, they lose their meaning. The reactor produces a Dark Matter Crystal the way a star produces a planetary system — rarely, slowly, with enormous investment, and with the sense that something remarkable just happened.

## 12. Fantasy Weight — A First-Class Design System

**Fantasy weight** is the emotional reaction a player has to a substance's name and discovery moment. It is entirely distinct from chain value — a substance can be load-bearing in the progression graph and have zero fantasy weight, just as a substance can have enormous fantasy weight and sit at a dead end.

Fantasy weight is the primary filter for substance retention. Chain value determines whether you can cut a substance without breaking progression. Fantasy weight determines whether you *want* it in the game at all.

### The Screenshot Test

Ask one question about every substance: **"Would a player screenshot this discovery and post it?"**

- Antihydrogen: yes.  
- Steel: yes — familiar, powerful, a milestone that means something.  
- Sulfur trioxide: no — it is a textbook intermediate that exists only to become sulfuric acid.  
- Hydrogen chloride: no — it dissolves into "I made salt the hard way."

If a substance fails the screenshot test and is not irreplaceable in the chain, it must be compressed or removed.

### Fantasy Weight Hierarchy

| Level | Label | Description | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5 | **Iconic** | Player will remember this forever. Discovery feels like an event. | Gold, Steel, Antihydrogen, Philosopher's Stone, Dark Matter Crystal |
| 4 | **Memorable** | Player registers it as significant. Good name, clear emotional role. | Graphene, Metallic Hydrogen, Fusion Plasma, Bose-Einstein Condensate |
| 3 | **Functional** | Name communicates purpose clearly. Player tracks it without it feeling exciting. | Iron Oxide, Sulfuric Acid, Silicon Dioxide, Doped Silicon |
| 2 | **Invisible** | Technically necessary. Player does not notice or enjoy it. | Sulfur Trioxide, Nitrogen Dioxide, Phosphoric Acid, Calcium Carbonate |
| 1 | **Dead Weight** | No emotional identity. No chain necessity. Player wonders why it exists. | Argon, Ozone, Zinc, Formaldehyde |

**Design rule:** Level 1 substances must be cut. Level 2 substances must be merged into their downstream product as a multi-reactant reagent. Level 3+ substances survive.

### How to Apply Fantasy Weight

1. **Rename first.** A substance at Level 2 can become Level 3 with a better name. Try the mineral name before the chemical name (`silicon dioxide → Quartz`, `calcium oxide → Quicklime`, `sodium carbonate → Soda Ash`).  
2. **Reframe second.** If renaming alone is insufficient, give the substance a distinct visual or hint identity that creates an emotional hook.  
3. **Merge or remove last.** If rename + reframe cannot lift the substance above Level 2, absorb it as a reaction reagent (multi-reactant synthesis) or cut it.

### Why Fantasy Weight Is Not Optional

A content universe that is optimized purely for chemical completeness produces a game where players spend fifteen minutes making sulfur trioxide. That is not a game. It is a chemistry worksheet with nicer graphics.

The reactor is a theatrical device. Every substance that appears on the inventory shelf is a character in a story the player is writing. If a substance would not appear in the climactic scene, it belongs backstage — as a reaction condition, not a discoverable item.

## 13. Naming Philosophy — How Substances Should Sound

Every substance name signals a generation and a register. Players learn the tone of the universe through naming before they understand the mechanics. Inconsistent naming — mythic substance names in Gen 1, textbook names in Gen 5 — destroys tonal coherence.

### The Three Naming Registers

| Register | Generations | Character | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Real** | Gen 1–2 | Recognizable, grounded. The player knows what this is. Familiarity is the fantasy. | Hydrogen, Iron, Salt, Gold, Ammonia, Bronze |
| **Elevated** | Gen 3–4 | Materials-science vocabulary. Scientific but not textbook. Implies precision and craft. | Graphene, Metallic Hydrogen, Ceramic Superconductor, Carbon Nanotube |
| **Mythic** | Gen 5–6 | Historical, classical, resonant. Named from physics history and alchemical tradition, not invented. | Fusion Plasma, Antihydrogen, Bose-Einstein Condensate, Philosopher's Stone, Aether |

### Six Naming Rules

1. **Use the mineral name before the chemical name.** `SiO₂ → Quartz`, `CaO → Quicklime`, `Na₂CO₃ → Soda Ash`, `Al₂O₃ → Corundum`. Chemical formulas belong in tooltips.  
2. **Use the popular name before the IUPAC name.** `C₆₀ → Buckyball`, `Cr → Chrome`, `YBCO → Ceramic Superconductor`. Players are not chemists.  
3. **Never invent words for Gen 6.** Every Gen 6 substance must be nameable from history (alchemy, classical physics, medieval natural philosophy). Invented portmanteaus belong in a different game.  
4. **Compound adjectives must add emotional weight, not technical description.** `Synthetic Diamond` (awe) beats `Diamond (Synthetic)` (footnote). `Ceramic Superconductor` (material, craft) beats `YBCO Superconductor` (acronym soup).  
5. **Abbreviations must be eliminatable.** Any substance whose display name requires explaining the acronym should be renamed. `YBCO` fails. `NiTi` fails. `BEC` passes only as a player shorthand for the full name.  
6. **Gen 6 names must survive being said aloud.** The player will say these names to other people. "I finally made the Philosopher's Stone" should sound like the most important thing that happened today.
